My Journey

My journey into fitness didn’t start from a place of strength — it started with pain. I spent long hours sitting in front of a computer, which led to persistent lower back pain. At the same time, I was eager to get stronger and started working out, but I had no real knowledge of resistance training.

Because of that, I avoided important exercises like leg training, and after about two years of lifting with poor technique and no proper understanding, I ended up with a shoulder injury. At that point, I realised that training without knowledge is not just ineffective — it’s dangerous.

That’s when I decided to take things seriously. I studied the fundamentals of resistance training, biomechanics, and proper movement. I also worked as a personal trainer to gain real-world coaching experience and understand how different bodies move and adapt.

Through structured training and proper technique, I was able to fix my lower back pain and regain my shoulder strength. More importantly, I learned how to train safely and effectively.

Now, my goal is simple — to help others avoid the same mistakes. This hypertrophy introduction page is built to guide beginners so they can build muscle, improve posture, and stay injury-free from the start.

Core Principles

1. Progressive Overload

Gradual increase of stress placed upon the body during exercise.

2. Full Range of Motion

Performing exercises through the entire movement potential of a joint.

3. Control & Tension

Focusing on the lowering phase and avoiding use of momentum.

4. Recovery & Rest

Ensuring adequate sleep and nutrition for muscle repair.

Anterior Pelvic Tilt (APT)

Excessive forward tilt of the pelvis, often caused by tight hip flexors and weak glutes/abs. It places high stress on the lower back during heavy lifts.
