Core Principles

1. Progressive Overload

The gradual increase of stress placed upon the body during exercise.

  • Increase weight
  • Increase repetitions

2. Full Range of Motion (ROM)

Performing an exercise through the entire movement potential of a joint.

Key benefit: Training at longer muscle lengths is superior for hypertrophy.

3. Controlling the Movement

Focusing on the eccentric (lowering) phase and avoiding the use of momentum.

Goal: Maximize time under tension.

4. Recovery & Consistency

Ensuring adequate sleep and nutrition while maintaining a long-term routine.

Success: Muscle growth happens during rest, not in the gym.
